Our Story

A taste passed down with care

Rosalie's Kakanin began the way most good kakanin does — in a home kitchen, with rice soaked overnight, banana leaves washed and warmed over a flame, and a recipe that's been adjusted by feel for years before it was ever written down.

Today, every tray of bibingka, every block of sapin-sapin, and every slice of biko leaving our kitchen in Patubig, Marilao is still made the same way it always has been: slowly, by hand, using real coconut milk and ingredients we'd be happy to serve our own family.

— Rosalie & the Kakanin team
